Japanese Car Legends

Toyota

Toyota is the global benchmark for durability and engineering logic. Built on the philosophy of Kaizen (continuous improvement), every vehicle is a masterclass in longevity. From world-first supercars to the hybrids that changed the planet, Toyota doesn't just build cars—it builds legacies.

Toyota 2000GT

Powered by a 2.0L inline-six engine (jointly developed with Yamaha)producing 150 hp.

⭐⭐⭐⭐☆

Toyota Supra

Famous for the fourth-generation (A80) model and its legendary 2JZ-GTE engine, known for its incredible durability and power. The latest GR Supra (A90) brings a focused 50:50 weight distribution and a turbocharged powertrain for modern precision.

⭐⭐⭐⭐☆

Toyota Crown 2023

An innovative "crossover-sedan" body style with an aggressive front fascia and available 21-inch wheels. Features the high-performance Hybrid MAX powertrain, delivering a combined 340 hp.

⭐⭐⭐⭐☆

Nissan

Nissan is where high-performance adrenaline meets accessible innovation. It is a brand that respects its "Hakosuka" roots while pushing the boundaries of what a digital, connected driving experience should feel like.

Nissan Skyline 1971

Powered by the legendary S20 2.0L inline-six, featuring double overhead cams (DOHC)—highly advanced for its time. Delivered 160 hp at 7,000 RPM, providing a high-revving, mechanical driving experience.

⭐⭐⭐⭐☆

Nissan GTR

Equipped with a hand-built VR38DETT 3.8L twin-turbo V6 engine, producing upwards of 565 hp (and much more in NISMO trims). Features the advanced ATTESA E-TS All-Wheel Drive system, which can shift torque between wheels in milliseconds for maximum grip.

⭐⭐⭐⭐☆

Nissan Z 2023

Powered by a 3.0L twin-turbo V6 engine pumping out 400 hp and 350 lb-ft of torque. Available with a classic 6-speed manual with SynchroRev Match or a lightning-fast 9-speed automatic.

⭐⭐⭐⭐☆

Honda

Honda is more than a car manufacturer; it is a company built on the "The Power of Dreams." From their legendary high-revving VTEC engines to their dominance in Formula 1, Honda has always been the brand for those who appreciate mechanical purity.

Honda S800

A remarkable 791cc inline-four engine capable of screaming to a 10,000 RPM redline, a testament to Honda’s motorcycle heritage. It produced 70 hp, allowing the lightweight car to reach speeds of 160 km/h, making it the fastest 1-liter production car of its time.

⭐⭐⭐⭐☆

Honda Civic

producing roughly 180 hp and 177 lb-ft of torque. It uses a small, fast-spooling turbo to provide low-end power while maintaining high MPG. 2.0L VTEC Turbo (Type R): The heart of the Type R produces over 315 hp, making it one of the most powerful 4-cylinder engines in its class.

⭐⭐⭐⭐☆

Honda Accord 2023

Features a fourth-generation hybrid system that is more powerful and refined, delivering a combined 204 hp. A minimalist, premium cabin featuring a 12.3-inch touchscreen—the largest in Honda history—with built-in Google integration.

⭐⭐⭐⭐☆

Mazda

Mazda is the boutique artist of the automotive world, driven by the philosophy of "Jinba Ittai"—the sacred bond between a rider and their horse. While other brands focus on mass production, Mazda focuses on the Takumi (artisan) spirit, treating every car as a sculpture brought to life through their award-winning Kodo: Soul of Motion design language.

Mazda Cosmo 1967

A tiny but mighty 982cc twin-rotor engine that produced 110 hp —an incredible output for its small size. With a high redline of 7,000RPM, it could reach a top speed of 185 km/h, proving that rotary engines were viable for high-performance driving.

⭐⭐⭐⭐☆

Mazda RX-7

Featuring a complex sequential twin-turbo system. Known for the 13B-REW engine, which provided linear power delivery and a unique exhaust note that is instantly recognizable to enthusiasts. A front mid-engine, rear-wheel-drive layout that gave it surgical precision on mountain roads and race tracks alike.

⭐⭐⭐⭐☆

Mazda MX-5 2024

Powered by a 2.0L Skyactiv-G engine delivering 181 hp, optimized for a 7,500 RPM redline.
